Depreciation

The allocation of the cost of the noncurrent asset over its useful life. It spreads the depreciable value of the asset over the years in which it will assist in generating revenue.

---

Accumulated depreciation

The value of the noncurrent asset that has been consumed so far. It is classified as a -NCA and is deducted from the historical cost of the asset in the balance sheet.

---

Carrying value

- The value of the noncurrent asset that is yet to be allocated as expense plus residual value.

- It represents the future economy benefit yet to be consumed by current and future owners.
- It is calculated by deducting the accumulated depreciation from the historical cost of the asset.

---

Depreciable asset

- NCA
- have a finite life
- is depreciated over its life

Whereas, non depreciable:

- infinite life, never used up (eg land)

Accrual basis purpose of depreciation (3 marks corrected)

Accrual basis (1) Because a NCA is not fully used in one period, depreciation spreads the cost of the asset over its useful life so that only the part of the asset consumed in the current reporting period is reported and matched against its revenue earnt in order to calculate an accurate net profit.

dep'n- relevance in ACCOUNTING REPORTS + why reported at carrying val

Including depreciation is useful for decision making.

In income statement ensures that the revenue the asset earnt in the period is matched against its expenses incurred in order to calculate an accurate net profit.

In balance sheet ensures that assets are reported at carrying value which is useful for decision making as it shows the owner if the asset is nearing the end of its useful life informing their decisions about the asset's replacement.
