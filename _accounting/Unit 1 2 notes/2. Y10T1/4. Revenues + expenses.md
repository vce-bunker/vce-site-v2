*chapter 10*
Revenue: An inflow of economic benefit (or saving in an outflow) in the form of an increase in assets (or decrease in liabilities) that increases owner's equity (net profit) and is not a capital contribution.

Expense: An outflow or consumption of an economic benefit (or saving in an inflow) in the form of a decrease in assets (or increase in liabilities) that decreases owner's equity (net profit) and is not drawings

Profit:
A profit for a period is accurately calculated by deducting the expenses incurred from the revenues earned.

Cash position:
Cash position for a period is accurately calculated by deducting cash outflows from cash inflows
