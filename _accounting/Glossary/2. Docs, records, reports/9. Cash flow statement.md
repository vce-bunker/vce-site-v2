Explain the role of the cash flow statement

The cash flow statement reports cash inflows and cash outflows, from operating, investing or financing, and the change int he firm’s bank balance over a period. {Cash information is more useful if it classifies common sources and uses of cash, and separately identifies their effect on the bank balance.}

Uses of the cash flow statement
- to aid decision making about the firm’s cash activities by detailing the sources and useds of cash in a particular period
- to assess whether or not the businbess is meeting its cash targets by comparing the Cash Flow Statement against budgeted (or expected) cash flows, which will highlight problems and allow for corrective action to be taken
- to assist in planning for future cash activities by providing a basis for the enxt budgeted Cash Flow Statement, which will set targets for the future
- to identify whether or not the bueiness is generating enough cash from its Operating Activities to fund its Investing and Financing activities
