Define the terms depreciation, accumulated depreciation, carrying value, residual value

Depreciation- the allocation of the cost of the noncurrent asset over its useful life. it spreads the depreciable value of the noncurrent asset over the years in which it will be used to generate revenue. It is classified as an expense and included under “Other expense” in the Income statement.

Accumulated depreciation- The value of the non-current asset that has been consumed so far. It is classified as a negative noncurrent asset and is deducted from the historical cost of the non-current asset in the balance sheet.

Carrying value- The value of the noncurrent asset that is yet to be consumed/allocated as an expense, plus residual value. It is calculated by deducting the Accumulated depreciation from Historical cost of the noncurrent asset.

Residual value- the value of the noncurrent asset at the end of its useful life.
