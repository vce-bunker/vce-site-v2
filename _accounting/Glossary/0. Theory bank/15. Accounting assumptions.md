Period assumption

- Reports are prepared for a particular period of time to obtain comparability of results
- Revenues are recognised in the period in which they are earned and expenses in the period they are incurred in order to calculate an accurate net profit for that reporting period

Accural basis

- Revenue is recognised in the period that it is earned and expenses when incurred regardless of when the cash is received/paid in order to calculate an accurate net profit.

Going concern

- Reports are prepared on the assumption that the business will continue to operate in the future.

Entity

- Records of assets, liabilities and business activities are kept completely separate from the owner and other businesses
