Nucleic acids-
- main forms: DNA and RNA
- organic polymers (or information molecules) that store genetic information and provide the instructions for the synthesis of proteins produced within an organism
- help form a particular characteristic of the organism


- **DNA** (_deoxyribo_ nucleic acid) the master copy of all genetic instructions, stored in nucleus of eukaryotic cells
- **RNA** (_ribo_ nucleic acid) temporary copy that transfers the genetic instructions from the nucleus to the rest of the cell

**Messenger RNA _(mRNA)_** – a transcripted copy of a DNA sequence (which codes for synthesis of a protein) brings genetic info from dna to ribosome

**Transfer RNA _(tRNA)_** – carries the amino acids to ribosome (for synthesis)
- contains 3 nucleotide bsaed sequence **(anticodon)** (see bottom part of image) which allows complimentary bonding w/ a mRNA codon, determines which amino acid is attached

**Ribosomal RNA _(rRNA)_** – mon structural component of the ribosome+responsible for its catalytic activity- provides a scaffold for the mrna to attach to

Genetic instruction (within mRNA) 
→ Protein ingredients (brought by tRNA) 
→ Manufacture of protein (catalysed by rRNA)

*transcription*- DNA codes for RNA (in nucleus)
*translation*- RNA codes for protein (at ribosomes/rough ER)
