- **Case study**
investigate event or problem in real world context
- **Classification and identification**
*classification- arrangement of phenomena, objects or events into sets*
identification- recognition of phenomena as belonging to particular sets
- **Controlled experiment**
investigate relationship between independent and dependent variable, other variables controlled (**kept consistent**)
- **Correlational study**
  *observation and recording of events and behaviours without manipulating or controlling them to **understand relationships between variables** (eg recording exercise and happiness levels and comparing relationship)*
- **Fieldwork**
  observing/sampling/interviewing and interacting with an environment beyond the classroom
- **Literature review**
  *Collation and analysis of secondary data to help explain observed events*
- **Modelling**
  construction of a physical/conceptual/mathematical model
- **Product, process or system development**
  design of an artefact, process or system to meet a human need
- **Simulation**
  Using models to study behaviour of a real or theoretical system
