- The conclusion links your evidence from the experiment to your hypothesis and the context of the investigation.
- The conclusion should state the main investigation result and whether the hypothesis was supported or not (NOT proven/correct or disproven/incorrect).
- This should be justified using specific details selected from the investigation findings. The significance of the results should be discussed in terms of how they link to relevant biological concepts and current scientific understanding.
- In drawing conclusions, they should briefly examine patterns, trends and relationships between variables with the limitations of the data in mind.
- Conclusions drawn from data must be limited by, and not go beyond, the data available.

- A **summary** of what you can **deduce** from the **results** of the investigation, including whether the tested hypothesis was **supported.**
- Use your observations to **answer the aim**.
- **Describe** overall **trends**.
- No reference to specific data (unless the aim requires it).
- Should not introduce any information that has not already been discussed in the results and discussion section.
