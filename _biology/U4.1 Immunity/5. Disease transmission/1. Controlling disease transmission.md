##### Scientific and social strategies

Scientific strategies
- **evidence based interventions that reduce transmission and infection of a pathogen.**
- eg vaccination, washing hands, using mosquito nets, insect repellants, face masks
- decontamination of high risk regions, vaccination programs

Social strategies
- **policies that affect groups of people, ensuring effective implementation of scientific strategies**
- eg 
- ensuring that there is appropriate **funding** available to quickly and effectively identify the pathogen and/or mode of transmission
- **education** campaigns about the proper use of medications such as antibiotics helps to reduce microbial resistance
- **signs** + education to tell people how to properly wash hands or promoting face masks
- **social distancing** (policy)
- **quarantining** (policy)
- environmental surveillance
