##### Methods of identifying pathogens
-  **Physical**: Determine bacteria structure through microscope
-  **Phenotypic**: Grow and test/identify bacteria through agar & chemical reactions
-  **Immunological**: Detect presence of antibodies or antigens (e.g. ELISA)
-  **Molecular**: Check genes with hybridisation or genome sequencing


##### Elisa
Sandwich method
- antibodies that are fixed to a plate trap specific antigens (that are placed in as serum sample after)
- Following this,Â detection antibodies (linked to colour changing enzymes) are added to provide positive identification

Highly sensitive and accurate

Direct
- antigen, then labeled antibody (less sensitive and accurate than sandwich)

Indirect method
- antigen, then antibody from serum, then a labelled antibody
- detects presence of antibody
